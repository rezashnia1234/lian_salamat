Apollo Hospital Chennai

Apollo Hospital Chennai was the first medical institute established by the Apollo Group, in 1983. The Apollo Hospital Chennai is the pioneer for many revolutionary technologies and procedures in India including: total knee replacement, coronary angioplasty, stereotactic radiotherapy, Birmingham Hip Resurfacing Procedure.

This hospital is the first hospital in South India to receive accreditation from the Joint Commission International, USA. It is recognized as a ‘Center of Excellence’ by the Indian Government. Furthermore, Apollo Chennai is the first hospital in India to de awarded ISO 9002 and ISO 14001 certifications. The Week Magazine has rated it as the “Best Private Sector Hospital in India”.

The Apollo Hospital shows statistics of 70% and 99.6% success rates in bone marrow transplants and heart surgeries respectively.

The hospital has an International Patient Services Department which is located on the fourth floor of the hospital’s Main block. The hospital offers interpretation services for major languages to assist in easy and convenient communication. The hospital offers a wide range of in-patient accommodation from General Wards to Suites.

Centers of Excellence

Apollo Hospitals has dedicated Centers of Excellence for several key specialties and super specialties. Cardiac Surgery, Bariatric Surgery, Oncology, Urology, Surgical Orthopedics and General Surgery all are dedicated Centers of Excellence. As such, Apollo Chennai has achieved several milestones including:

Apollo Hospitals, Chennai completes the highest number of Combined Heart and Double Lung Transplants in India.

The Apollo Institutes of Orthopedics are the first in Asia to offer Robotic Spine Surgery, Robotic rehabilitation (Lokomat - a robotic treadmill and Erigo Robotic Tilt Table).

Apollo Hospitals, Chennai successfully performed a rare complex intervention procedure on an 80-year-old man who had a giant abdominal aortic aneurysm (AAA).

First donor incompatible kidney transplant performed at Apollo Hospitals Chennai using technique of Column adsorption of blood group antibodies.

In less than three years since inception, the team at the Apollo Centre for Liver Disease and Transplantation, Chennai completed over 100 Liver Transplantations in June 2011, marking a noteworthy first-of-its-kind milestone in the city of Chennai.

Apollo Specialty Hospital, Chennai is one of the few centers in India with facilities to search for a potential unrelated donor and offer transplantation. Also, In fact, the hospital has been instrumental in performing 50 such procedures over the past few years thus offering a unique opportunity for patients to get cured.

Obesity is not a cosmetic issue, it’s a health issue.

Obesity is described as the excessive accumulation of fat that exceeds the body's skeletal and physical standards. Taking into account the total number of afflicted individuals, obesity constitutes a significant disorder in the world.

Obesity is measured by body mass index (BMI). BMI grades obesity into overweight (22.5-27.4), obese (27.5 – 32.4), super obese (32.5 – 37.4) & morbid obesity (more than 37.5 or 40 kg over ideal weight). It becomes a serious health risk when it becomes morbid. Persons living with morbid obesity have 50 – 100% higher mortality rates than the one with a healthy weight, including more than 50% higher cancer mortality rate.The good news is that most obesity-related health problems improve, and some are even cured, after 70 – 80% of excess weight loss.

According to international criteria, the indications for obesity surgery are BMI >35 or BMI>32.5 with pronounced co-morbidities. Nevertheless, limits to the indication for surgery continue to be changing.
